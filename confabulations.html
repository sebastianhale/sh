Crowds of sculpted stone birds on top of roofs
Posted on July 2, 2022

By the window listening to the rolling autumn rain. I let my tea go cold, that's not a good feeling. It's still okay.
It rained so much I had to wait inside a café while cars were flying past and people fled with umbrellas. In the afternoon though, I got to go to the top of a dome to draw from the vantage point lofty places are ; I took the drawing book with the marbled cover. A few days ago I was with a friend who explained each item in a sketchbook, and a collection of pictures of reference. I wish I could buy published and bound books like that. Of images, related through intuition and taste and forming a symphony of sorts, with some words. A notebook, really is what I mean. Something which is an expression of the maker's sensitivity to pictures and words, and what they think should be recorded, without the finish that most books have, or the perceived necessity to make it all cohere, a manual of colour and style.
I waited for the bus. I had left the window open and it was cold when I came back, some rain had fallen inside, some paper had blown away from where it was before, but everything was intact.


Tuesday Vows
Posted on June 21, 2022

I went through an avenue of trees this morning which was cut across by a railway line, then continued on the other side. I've had a good day away from the usual things.

I dreamt that I was sticking up notices on windows with green frames in a corner of a square. In the street was someone I went to school with who I barely knew at all and a tall man singing in an incomprehensible language.
There are often these street-like courtyards in my dreams. One time I had a meeting with someone in a vast square of gorgeous old buildings which backed onto a park. I cycled from busy streets into the square and waited for them on a low wall.
More recently I was in such a square in London specifically, waiting for a wedding some hours away. Nicely-dressed people moved awkwardly around, waiting. A dog watched from the shade; only she, of all the occupants of this dream, seemed to be alive. Everything was still and quiet, in anticipation or apprehension. There was a bookcase in the middle of the street, stuffed with books that were falling apart. The deterioration was deliberate; they weren't very old books at all. I was having a conversation about them with two people older than me who held champagne glasses, but unfortunately I have forgotten which book it was that I took from the shelf while I spoke.

On my way back home it was getting dark, I looked into the warm glowing windows behind black garden railings at the old faces at their desks.

Amirador
Posted on May 16, 2022

My remarkable friend has an amazing flat which is disorganised in a good way; there are two desks, both of dark wood, one in a very small alcove with a window, mismatching chairs and armchairs in the middle of the room with an antique coffee table, all atop a faded rug, and a bluish-grey sofa close by. Beside one desk, pinned vertically up the wall are drawings, old ones mixed with new ones, mostly of flowers. Her drawings suffice anyway but she doesn't actually keep real flowers, and if she does for some reason, they end up dead and wilted until I or someone else visits and gets rid of them.

The room has blue patterned wallpaper for the most part but the wall with the door is of a different paper. Bookshelves in the corner; almost floor to ceiling, on the corner face of each wall. And this arrangement is mirrored diagonally opposite in the corner on the right entering the room. Wallpaper from there across to the window above the sofa. Lots of books stacked on the floor, old lamps with fabric shades. The opposite wall to the one with the recessed-desk has two windows with thick curtains, there's another chair looking out one of them, with clothes thrown across it, like most of the furniture. Mismatched pillows, she sleeps on the sofa (doesn't believe in beds) so there are blankets always strewn across it, and books underneath it.

And she acts like it isn't anything special but she must have accumulated the things for years. She has contrasted all of these styles and it works so well. Entering from the door, there is another small wing on the left, which I am in now, the wallpaper is different and here is the small kitchen. The roof, which is wooden, slants and the windows here have frames that match the roof wood (the others are painted white). The bathroom is attached to here, with cream-beige walls and black and white tiles and very singular light fittings.

Paper Raincoat
Posted on April 22, 2022

It had just stopped raining in a dark city; very dark, only a few lamp-like windows. I was walking up low, stone steps that made no logical sense, then back down a slope into a carpark overgrown with wet elm trees. Empty fields of grey and brown were beyond it.

Another dream involved an installation in a dream-library. A display which formed sphinxes and obelisks from levitating strands of light and matter, moving outwards from the back of the room, over bookshelves and our heads. The room was at an unbearable slant. I was watching with the architect of this installation, she was showing it off proudly, like artists do.

There is a brown tree with yellow leaves in a crumbled courtyard that I keep walking past. I will get a picture.

It is autumn, the best time of year, “the king of seasons” with the best colours. Autumn and the late afternoon are inextricable. They're the same sensation, the same colours.


Gravestone
Posted on March 31, 2022

I asked him what he thought of the pictures I brought him, but before I finished he begged; ‘Do you hear that?’
‘Hear what?’ I replied.
‘Three dead men riding north,’ he said.

Once I found a man in a quiet dark room, far from the world that I had come from, sitting bent on the corner of a tomb. Around him were trinkets and golden cups, shields, old intricate instruments, gilded books, a seemly accumulation of treasure. He couldn't turn to face me, he just asked quietly, ‘have you come to rob me?’ I said that I had not. Then he thought he knew me; he might have supposed that if I hadn’t come to rob him I might be his saviour; he said he was surprised to hear my voice. I took a step closer, and saw better the glittering treasure but was unmoved by it. I had everything I could ever want in the Greenhouse downstairs. I asked whether he had prepared himself for the afterlife with this elaborate display, but he said no: ‘I just thought to arrange these together. That was some time ago now. Tell me, what is it like there?’ I asked him where exactly he meant. ‘The other world,’ he replied. ‘Tell me.’
‘The other world is evanescent and withered,’ I said reflexively. He seemed very disappointed.
‘Do you know,’ he said with effort, ‘I have dreamed of late of ladders made of thorns.’ I was surprised; ‘so have I!’ I said.
‘And did you climb them?’ he pressed.
‘No, but my companion did. They took pleasure from it.’ He didn't respond. ‘I ran away,’ I added. ‘That afternoon, I was close to the other world. I could hear its discordance.’
He somehow became even more bent and crooked and sighed for what felt like a very long time.
